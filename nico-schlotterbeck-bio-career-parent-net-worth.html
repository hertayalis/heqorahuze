Nico Schlotterbeck Bio

Nico Cedric Schlotterbeck is a professional footballer from Germany who plays as a centre-back for Bundesliga club Borussia Dortmund and the Germany national team.

Schlotterbeck’s uncle is the former professional footballer, who played for Freiburg— Neils Schlotterbeck. His older brother, Keven, is a professional footballer who played the 2019/2020 season at Union Berlin on loan from SC Freiburg, to which he returned for the 2020/2021 season. 

Growing up, Nico has enjoyed the protection by his older brother, Kevin Schlotterbeck.

Nico Schlotterbeck Career

Nico started his youth career with VfR Aalen. He played with the team till 2015 where he moved to Karlsruher SC. He played for Karlsruher for 2 years before moving to Freiburg.

Schlotterbeck made his debut for SC Freiburg on 9th March 2019, coming on as a half-time substitute for Philipp Lienhart in the 2–1 home win against Hertha BSC. In his final match for Freiburg, the club lost the DFB-Pokal Final on penalties but Schlotterbeck was awarded the man of the match.

Schlotterbeck joined Union Berlin on a one-year loan on 31st July 2020. On 2nd May 2022, Borussia Dortmund announced the signing of Schlotterbeck on a five-year deal, starting from the 2022–23 season. He joined for a reported €25 million transfer fee.

For his international side, Schlotterbeck got his debut call-up to the Germany national team under coach Hansi Flick for FIFA World Cup qualification in September 2021, He made his debut against Israel in a friendly on 27th March 2022.
